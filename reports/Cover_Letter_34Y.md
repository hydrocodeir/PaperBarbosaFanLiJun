Please consider our manuscript entitled **"Beyond Mean Warming: Quantile-Dependent Thermal-Extreme Change, Uncertainty, and Regionalization Across Iran"** for publication in *`[Journal Name]`*.

This study presents an observation-based assessment of recent thermal-extreme change across Iran using records from 124 meteorological stations for 1991-2024. Rather than focusing only on mean change, we examine how lower, central, and upper quantiles of four annual thermal-extreme indices have evolved across the country. To do so, we combine station-wise quantile regression with dependent-bootstrap uncertainty estimation, false-discovery-rate field-significance screening, spatial diagnostics, and cluster-based regionalization. The results show that recent thermal-extreme change across Iran is not distributionally uniform: warm days exhibit the clearest upper-tail amplification, warm nights increase more evenly across the distribution, and cool extremes contract broadly, especially in cool days.

We believe the manuscript is well suited to *`[Journal Name]`* for two main reasons. First, it provides new evidence from a climatically diverse and societally important region where dense station-based assessments of distributional change in thermal extremes remain relatively limited. Second, it offers a methodological contribution that extends beyond the regional case itself by integrating quantile-based trend analysis, uncertainty quantification, field significance, and regionalization within a single reproducible framework. We expect the manuscript to be of interest to readers working on climate extremes, regional climate change, climatological methods, and heat-risk assessment.
