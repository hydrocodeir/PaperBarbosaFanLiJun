Please consider our manuscript, **"Quantile-dependent changes in warm and cool temperature extremes across Iran during 1961-2024,"** for publication in the *International Journal of Climatology*.

This study presents a station-based assessment of four annual thermal-extreme indices across Iran using observations from 30 meteorological stations over 1961-2024. Rather than relying only on mean trends, we examine the full distribution of change using quantile regression, and we complement the analysis with bootstrap uncertainty estimation, field-significance testing, regionalization, and methodological sensitivity assessment.

The principal contribution of the manuscript is that it shows thermal-extreme change over Iran is not adequately described as a uniform shift in the mean. Warm days and warm nights increase broadly, but with quantile- and station-dependent amplification, while cool days and cool nights decline most strongly in upper quantiles, indicating a rapid loss of years historically characterized by many cool events. The results also show that these changes are regionally heterogeneous and only weakly represented by smooth national-scale spatial autocorrelation, highlighting the importance of local and regional climatic controls.

We believe the manuscript fits the aims of the *International Journal of Climatology* because it uses a regional observational dataset to address a broader climatological question of international relevance: how the distribution of thermal extremes is being reorganized in an arid, topographically complex environment. The study also contributes methodologically by demonstrating the added value of quantile-based diagnostics for interpreting observed climate extremes beyond mean-trend analyses.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the manuscript and agree with its submission to the journal.
