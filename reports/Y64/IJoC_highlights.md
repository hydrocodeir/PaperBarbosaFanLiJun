- Quantile regression reveals that thermal-extreme change across Iran is strongly distribution-dependent rather than mean-uniform.
- Warm days and warm nights increase widely, with pronounced upper-tail amplification at several stations.
- Cool days and cool nights decline most strongly in upper quantiles, implying rapid loss of historically cool years.
- Field significance is strongest at intermediate quantiles, but Moran's I indicates weak national-scale spatial autocorrelation.
- The principal findings remain robust across alternative reference periods, bootstrap methods, and interpolation choices.
